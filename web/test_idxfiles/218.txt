<h1><a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2011/12/04/arts/design/alexander-garvin-looks-at-public-spaces-in-new-york.html?ref=design">
Treasuring Urban Oases</a></h1>
<h6 class="byline">
By MICHAEL KIMMELMAN        			<span class="timestamp">8:00 AM ET</span>
</h6>
</div>
<div class="ledePhoto">
<div class="image">
<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2011/12/04/arts/design/alexander-garvin-looks-at-public-spaces-in-new-york.html?ref=design"><img src="http://graphics8.nytimes.com/images/2011/12/04/arts/04SUBSPACES_SPAN/04SUBSPACES_SPAN-sfSpan.jpg" border="0" width="395" height="263" alt="The fountain at Bryant Park in Midtown." /></a>
</div>
<h6 class="credit">Richard Perry/The New York Times</h6>
<p class="caption">The fountain at Bryant Park in Midtown.</p>
</div>
<p class="summary">
Alexander Garvin, an architect and urban planner, has spent the better part of the last half-century thinking about New York City’s public spaces.</p>
<ul class="refer">
<li><a href="http://www.nytimes.com/slideshow/2011/12/04/arts/design/20111204-SPACES.html"><img src="http://graphics8.nytimes.com/images/multimedia/icons/photo_icon.gif" alt="Photographs" width="12" height="10" border="0"> Walking New York's Public Spaces</a></li>
</ul>

<h3><a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2011/12/02/arts/design/storytelling-in-japanese-art-at-the-met-review.html?ref=design">
Unfurling a Thousand Years of Gods, Demons and Romance</a></h3>
<h6 class="byline">
By ROBERTA SMITH 
</h6>
<p class="summary">
“Storytelling in Japanese Art” at the Metropolitan Museum of Art reveals the narrative side of Japanese art in a lavish assortment of hand scrolls, hanging scrolls, screens and books.</p>
<ul class="refer">
<li><a href="http://www.nytimes.com/slideshow/2011/12/02/arts/design/20111202_STORYTELLING.html"><span class="icon slideshow">Slide Show</span></a></li>

</ul>
</div>
